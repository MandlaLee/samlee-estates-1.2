Areas Near UFS

Students studying at UFS often look for accommodation in areas that support access to campus, transport routes, shops, libraries and daily student routines.

Which areas are popular for UFS students?
UFS students often look at areas such as Universitas, Brandwag, Park West, Wesdene and other nearby student-friendly areas in Bloemfontein.
Is Universitas a good area for UFS students?
Universitas is commonly associated with UFS student life because it is one of the key areas students consider when looking for accommodation near UFS.

No. Distance is important, but students should also check safety, cost, WiFi, facilities, house rules, transport access and study environment.

What if I am not sure which area to choose?

Room and Facility Questions for UFS Students

What room type should UFS students choose?
The best room type depends on your budget, privacy needs, funding type and study habits. Some students prefer private rooms, while others choose shared rooms for affordability.
Can UFS students choose private rooms?
Students can indicate a private room preference on the application form, but final options depend on availability.
Can UFS students choose shared rooms?
Yes. Students can indicate a shared room preference if they are looking for a more affordable or social student living option.
What facilities should UFS students ask about?
Students should ask about WiFi, study space, kitchen access, bathroom access, laundry, safety, electricity, water and house rules.
Is WiFi important for UFS students?
Yes. WiFi is important for assignments, research, online learning, communication and university administration.
Should UFS students ask about house rules?
Yes. House rules help manage visitors, noise, shared spaces, cleanliness and general student conduct.

Safety Questions for UFS Students

What safety questions should UFS students ask before moving in?
Ask about access control, locks, lighting, visitor rules, emergency contacts, house rules and how issues are reported.
Should parents ask about safety?
Yes. Parents should ask about location, access rules, visitor policies, transport routes, emergency contacts and how the accommodation is managed.
Should students save emergency contacts?
Yes. UFS students should save emergency contacts, accommodation contacts, family contacts and university support contacts on their phones.
Should students walk alone at night?
Students should avoid unsafe routes, plan transport carefully, follow local safety advice and communicate with trusted people when travelling.
How can students protect their belongings?
Students should lock rooms, protect valuables, avoid sharing keys or access codes and report security concerns early.
Where can I read more about student safety?
You can read the SamLee Estates student safety guide for Bloemfontein.

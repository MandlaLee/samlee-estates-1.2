General NSFAS Questions

What is NSFAS?
NSFAS stands for the National Student Financial Aid Scheme. It helps eligible students at public universities and TVET colleges with study-related funding support.
Does NSFAS fund accommodation?
NSFAS may support accommodation for qualifying students where the accommodation meets the required approval or accreditation process.
Can NSFAS students stay in private accommodation?
Yes. NSFAS-funded students may stay in approved or accredited private accommodation where the required processes are followed.
Why is NSFAS accreditation important?
Accreditation helps confirm that accommodation meets required student housing standards and is suitable for funded students.
Is SamLee Estates NSFAS accredited?
Yes. SamLee Estates provides NSFAS accredited student accommodation in Bloemfontein.
Can first-year students apply for NSFAS accommodation?
Yes. First-year students who are NSFAS-funded should apply early and prepare their accommodation documents before registration pressure begins.

NSFAS Accommodation Questions

What is NSFAS accredited accommodation?
NSFAS accredited accommodation is student accommodation that has gone through the relevant approval or accreditation process for NSFAS-funded students.
Does accommodation need to be approved?
Students relying on NSFAS accommodation support should choose approved or accredited accommodation where required.
What should I check before choosing NSFAS accommodation?
Check accreditation, location, safety, house rules, WiFi, study space, transport access, facilities and the application process.
Can I choose accommodation near my campus?
Yes. Students should choose accommodation that supports access to campus, transport, libraries, lectures and daily routines.

NSFAS Accommodation Application Questions

How do I apply for NSFAS accommodation?
Start by applying for accommodation with the provider and make sure your funding, registration and accommodation documents are prepared.
When should I apply for NSFAS accommodation?
Apply as early as possible before registration season. Accommodation demand increases strongly before the academic year begins.
Can I apply before registration?
You can start preparing and making enquiries before registration, but some documents may only become available after registration is completed.
What information should I submit when applying?
Submit your full name, contact details, institution, funding type, preferred area, move-in date and any accommodation preferences.
What happens after I apply?
Your details are reviewed and you may be contacted about availability, documents, room options and next steps.
Can I apply online with SamLee Estates?
Yes. You can submit your accommodation application online through the SamLee Estates application page.

NSFAS Document Questions

What documents should NSFAS students prepare?
Students should prepare their ID, student number, admission information, proof of registration where available, NSFAS information and accommodation documents.
Do I need proof of registration?
Proof of registration may be required during the accommodation process, especially once your university registration has been completed.
Do I need a lease agreement?
A lease or accommodation agreement may be required as part of the accommodation process. Students should keep copies of all signed documents.
Should I keep digital copies of my documents?
Yes. Keep documents saved on your phone, email, laptop or cloud storage so you can access them when needed.
Can parents help prepare documents?
Yes. Parents and guardians can help students organise ID copies, registration documents, emergency contacts and accommodation information.
What if one document is missing?
Ask for guidance early. Missing documents can delay the accommodation process, especially during busy registration periods.

NSFAS Payment and Allowance Questions

How much does NSFAS pay for accommodation?
NSFAS allowance amounts and payment rules can change. Students should confirm the latest information directly through NSFAS and their institution.
Does NSFAS pay the student or the accommodation provider?
Payment arrangements may depend on institutional processes, NSFAS systems and accommodation arrangements. Students should confirm the process before moving in.
Can I receive accommodation and transport support at the same time?
Students should confirm their specific allowance situation with NSFAS and their institution, because accommodation and transport support depend on where and how the student lives.
What happens if NSFAS payment is delayed?
Students should communicate early with the accommodation provider and institution, keep all records and follow official NSFAS communication.
Should I budget even if I am NSFAS-funded?
Yes. NSFAS-funded students should still budget carefully for food, transport, data, toiletries, laundry, printing and emergencies.
Where can I read more about NSFAS accommodation allowances?
You can read the SamLee Estates NSFAS accommodation allowance guide for more student-friendly guidance.

NSFAS Parent Questions

Can parents help students choose NSFAS accommodation?
Yes. Parents can help students compare location, safety, facilities, documents, house rules and the accommodation process.
What should parents ask before choosing accommodation?
Parents should ask about safety, visitor rules, distance to campus, WiFi, electricity, water, laundry, emergency contacts and what is included in the cost.
Should parents check if accommodation is NSFAS accredited?
Yes. If a student is NSFAS-funded, parents should help confirm whether the accommodation is approved or accredited where required.
Can parents contact SamLee Estates?

Moving Into NSFAS Accommodation

What should I bring when moving into student accommodation?
Bring your documents, bedding, clothes, toiletries, study items, electronics, laundry items, basic kitchen items and personal essentials.
Should I confirm what is included before moving in?
Yes. Ask what furniture, WiFi, electricity, water, kitchen access, laundry access and shared facilities are included.
What should I do on move-in day?
Confirm your room, save emergency contacts, read house rules, check locks, unpack essentials and inform family that you arrived safely.
How can I prepare for student life?
Create a monthly budget, attend lectures, build a study routine, follow house rules, protect your valuables and ask for help early when needed.

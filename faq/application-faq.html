Application FAQ

Answers to common questions about applying for SamLee Estates student accommodation near UFS and CUT in Bloemfontein.

Applying for Student Accommodation

The application process helps SamLee Estates understand who you are, where you study, how you are funded, when you need accommodation and what type of room or area may suit you.

This page answers the most common questions students and parents ask before submitting an accommodation application.

Main Application Questions

How do I apply for SamLee Estates accommodation?
You can apply online by completing the SamLee Estates application form. The form asks for your name, contact details, institution, year of study, funding type, preferred room type, preferred area and move-in date.
Where is the application form?
The application form is on the SamLee Estates application page. You can access it by clicking Apply Online or Apply Now on the website.
What happens after I submit the application form?
Your details are submitted to SamLee Estates for review. The team can then use your information to understand your accommodation needs and contact you about availability or next steps.
Will my application be saved?
Yes. When you submit the online form, your application details are sent directly to SamLee Estates and recorded for follow-up.
Do I receive accommodation immediately after applying?
Submitting an application does not automatically mean a room is confirmed. Your details must first be reviewed, and availability or requirements may need to be confirmed.
Should I apply early?
Yes. Students should apply as early as possible because accommodation demand increases before registration season and the start of the academic year.

Details Needed When Applying

To make your application easier to process, prepare your information before filling in the form.

What personal details do I need to submit?
You need to submit your first name, last name, phone number and email address so SamLee Estates can identify you and contact you.
Why do I need to provide my institution?
Your institution helps SamLee Estates understand whether you need accommodation near UFS, near CUT or near another Bloemfontein study location.
Why do I need to provide my year of study?
Your year of study helps the team understand whether you are a first-year student, returning student, postgraduate student or another type of student.
Why do I need to provide my funding type?
Funding type matters because NSFAS-funded students, privately funded students, bursary students and parent-funded students may have different accommodation processes or document needs.
Do I need to choose a preferred area?
Choosing a preferred area helps SamLee Estates understand whether you prefer accommodation near UFS, near CUT, Universitas, Park West, Willows, Brandwag, Wesdene or another suitable area.
What should I write in extra details?
You can mention your campus, budget, NSFAS status, room preference, move-in urgency, parent contact details or anything important about your accommodation needs.

NSFAS Application Questions

Can NSFAS-funded students apply?
Yes. NSFAS-funded students can apply for SamLee Estates student accommodation in Bloemfontein.
Is SamLee Estates NSFAS accredited?
Yes. SamLee Estates provides NSFAS accredited student accommodation in Bloemfontein.
What should NSFAS students prepare before applying?
NSFAS-funded students should prepare their ID, student number, institution details, NSFAS information, proof of registration where available and any accommodation-related documents requested later.
Can I apply before NSFAS is fully confirmed?
You can make an accommodation enquiry early, but your funding and registration details may need to be confirmed before accommodation is finalised.
Should I mention my NSFAS status in the form?
Yes. Select NSFAS under funding type and use the extra details box if you need to explain your NSFAS situation.
Where can I read more about NSFAS accommodation?
You can read the NSFAS hub, NSFAS accredited accommodation page and NSFAS accommodation application guide on the SamLee Estates website.

Documents and Preparation Questions

Do I need documents before applying online?
You can submit the online application form with your basic details first. However, you should prepare documents because they may be needed during the follow-up process.
What documents may be needed later?
You may need your ID, proof of registration, student number, NSFAS information, parent or guardian details and any accommodation documents required during the process.
Do first-year students need proof of registration?
First-year students may only receive proof of registration after completing university registration. If you do not have it yet, prepare the documents you already have and update your details later if needed.
Should I keep copies of my documents?
Yes. Keep digital copies of your ID, admission information, registration documents, NSFAS details and accommodation communication.
Can my parent or guardian help me apply?
Yes. A parent or guardian can help you prepare your details, check information and ask questions, especially if you are a first-year student.
What if I make a mistake on the form?
If you submit incorrect details, contact SamLee Estates as soon as possible and explain what needs to be corrected.

Room and Area Preference Questions

Do I have to choose a room type?
You can choose a preferred room type if you already know what you want. If you are not sure, you can select any available option or leave more detail in the extra details box.
Can I choose private or shared accommodation?
Yes. The application form lets you indicate whether you prefer a private room, shared room, affordable option, NSFAS accredited option or any available option.
Can I apply for accommodation near UFS?
Yes. Students studying at the University of the Free State can apply for accommodation near UFS.
Can I apply for accommodation near CUT?
Yes. Students studying at the Central University of Technology can apply for accommodation near CUT.
Can I apply if I am not sure which area is best?
Yes. You can select any suitable area and provide more information in the extra details box.
Will I always get my preferred room type?
Room type depends on availability and suitability. Your preference helps guide the process, but final options may depend on what is available at the time.

After Submitting the Application

How will SamLee Estates contact me?
SamLee Estates can use the phone number or email address you provided in the application form to contact you.
How long does the application process take?
The process can depend on demand, availability, document readiness and the time of year. Applying early gives you more time to prepare.
Should I submit the form more than once?
Only submit the form again if your details have changed or you made a mistake. Too many duplicate submissions can make your application harder to track.
What if I do not hear back immediately?
Be patient during busy periods, but you can contact SamLee Estates if your accommodation need is urgent or your details have changed.
Can I contact SamLee Estates after applying?
Yes. You can use the contact page if you need to ask a question, update your details or follow up on your accommodation application.
Should I keep proof that I applied?
Yes. Keep any confirmation, screenshots or communication related to your application for your own records.

Parent and Guardian Questions

Can parents help with the application?
Yes. Parents and guardians can help students complete the form, prepare documents and understand accommodation requirements.
Can parents contact SamLee Estates directly?
Yes. Parents and guardians can contact SamLee Estates to ask about student accommodation, safety, location, applications and requirements.
What should parents check before accommodation is finalised?
Parents should check safety, area, house rules, transport access, costs, what is included, emergency contacts and whether the accommodation suits the student’s funding situation.
Should parents help students prepare documents?
Yes. Parents can help students organise ID copies, proof of registration, NSFAS information, parent contact details and emergency contacts.

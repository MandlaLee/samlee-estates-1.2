Quick Answers

Most Common Questions

Browse quick answers before visiting the full FAQ pages.

How do I apply for SamLee Estates accommodation?
Complete the online application form with your name, contact details, institution, year of study, funding type, preferred area, room type and move-in date.
Should I apply early?
Yes. Students should apply early because accommodation demand usually increases before registration season and the start of the academic year.
Can first-year students apply?
Yes. First-year students can apply and should prepare their documents, funding details and move-in needs early.
What happens after I submit the form?
SamLee Estates receives your details and can contact you about availability, documents, room options or the next step.
Is SamLee Estates NSFAS accredited?
Yes. SamLee Estates provides NSFAS accredited student accommodation in Bloemfontein.
Can NSFAS-funded students apply online?
Yes. NSFAS-funded students can apply online and select NSFAS as their funding type.
What should NSFAS students prepare?
Students should prepare ID details, institution details, student number, proof of registration where available and NSFAS funding information.
Can I apply before my NSFAS is fully complete?
You can make an accommodation enquiry early, but your registration and funding details may need to be confirmed before accommodation is finalised.
What facilities should I ask about?
Ask about WiFi, furniture, kitchen access, bathroom access, laundry, electricity, water, safety, study space and house rules.
Is WiFi important for student accommodation?
Yes. WiFi supports assignments, research, online learning, communication and student administration.
Should I ask what is included in the room?
Yes. Ask whether the room includes a bed, desk, chair, wardrobe, storage, curtains and other essentials.
Are shared spaces important?
Yes. Shared kitchens, bathrooms, laundry areas and common spaces affect comfort, routine, cleanliness and student living.
What safety questions should I ask?
Ask about locks, access control, lighting, visitor rules, emergency contacts, house rules and how issues are reported.
Should parents ask about safety?
Yes. Parents should ask about the area, access rules, visitor rules, emergency contacts, transport routes and property support.
Are house rules important?
Yes. House rules help manage visitors, noise, shared spaces, cleanliness and respectful student living.
Who should current tenants contact for property issues?
Current tenants should contact the caretaker linked to their property or use urgent property support where needed.
Can parents contact SamLee Estates?
Yes. Parents and guardians can contact SamLee Estates about applications, safety, location, costs, house rules and student support.
Can parents help students apply?
Yes. Parents can help students prepare documents, check details and complete the online application using the student’s correct information.
What should parents check before accommodation is finalised?
Parents should check safety, location, transport, costs, facilities, house rules, documents and emergency contact information.
Should parents keep records?
Yes. Parents and students should keep copies of applications, messages, agreements, proof of payments and important accommodation communication.
What costs should students budget for?
Students should budget for rent, food, transport, toiletries, laundry, printing, data and emergencies.
Should I ask what is included in rent?
Yes. Ask whether WiFi, water, electricity, furniture, laundry or other services are included or charged separately.
Should I keep proof of payments?
Yes. Always keep payment records, receipts, agreements and written communication related to accommodation.
How can students avoid cost surprises?
Ask for clear information about total costs, included services, deposits, payment dates, extra charges and refund conditions.

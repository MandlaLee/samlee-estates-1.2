Room and Facility Questions for CUT Students

What room type should CUT students choose?
The best room type depends on your budget, privacy needs, funding type and study habits. Some students prefer private rooms, while others choose shared rooms for affordability.
Can CUT students choose private rooms?
Students can indicate a private room preference on the application form, but final options depend on availability.
Can CUT students choose shared rooms?
Yes. Students can indicate a shared room preference if they are looking for a more affordable or social student living option.
What facilities should CUT students ask about?
Students should ask about WiFi, study space, kitchen access, bathroom access, laundry, safety, electricity, water and house rules.
Is WiFi important for CUT students?
Yes. WiFi is important for assignments, research, online learning, communication and institution administration.
Should CUT students ask about house rules?
Yes. House rules help manage visitors, noise, shared spaces, cleanliness and general student conduct.

Safety Questions for CUT Students

What safety questions should CUT students ask before moving in?
Ask about access control, locks, lighting, visitor rules, emergency contacts, house rules and how issues are reported.
Should parents ask about safety?
Yes. Parents should ask about location, access rules, visitor policies, transport routes, emergency contacts and how the accommodation is managed.
Should students save emergency contacts?
Yes. CUT students should save emergency contacts, accommodation contacts, family contacts and institution support contacts on their phones.
Should students walk alone at night?
Students should avoid unsafe routes, plan transport carefully, follow local safety advice and communicate with trusted people when travelling.
How can students protect their belongings?
Students should lock rooms, protect valuables, avoid sharing keys or access codes and report security concerns early.

You can read the SamLee Estates student safety guide for Bloemfontein.

Accommodation for First-Year Students

Starting university is one of life's biggest milestones. For many students, it is the first time living away from home, managing their own schedules and adapting to a new academic environment. Choosing the right accommodation can make this transition significantly easier.

First-year students benefit from accommodation that provides a balance between independence, safety, academic support and community. Living in a student-focused environment helps new students settle into university life more comfortably while building friendships and support networks.

Whether you are studying at the University of the Free State (UFS), the Central University of Technology (CUT) or another institution in Bloemfontein, selecting the right accommodation can positively influence your entire first year.

Why Accommodation Matters for First-Year Students

Your accommodation becomes your home while studying. The environment around you can influence your academic performance, wellbeing and overall university experience.

What First-Year Students Should Look For

Choosing accommodation can feel overwhelming. Focusing on a few key factors can help simplify the decision-making process.

  • Safe and secure accommodation
  • Reliable WiFi and internet access
  • Study-friendly rooms and environments
  • Convenient access to campus
  • Student-focused management
  • Laundry facilities
  • Access to shops and amenities
  • Positive student community

Near UFS and CUT

Many first-year students prefer accommodation close to their institution. Living near campus reduces travel time, makes attending lectures easier and helps students become more involved in university life.

Being closer to campus can also make it easier to access libraries, computer labs, sporting facilities and student support services.

Common First-Year Challenges

Almost every student experiences challenges during their first year. Adapting to academic expectations, managing time effectively and living independently can feel overwhelming at first.

The right accommodation environment can help students navigate these challenges by providing structure, support and opportunities to connect with other students facing similar experiences.

Tips for First-Year Students

  • Apply for accommodation early
  • Attend orientation activities
  • Create a study routine
  • Get involved in student life
  • Balance academics and social activities
  • Ask for help when needed
  • Prioritise your wellbeing
  • Build positive relationships with fellow students

Frequently Asked Questions

When should first-year students apply for accommodation?
Students should apply as early as possible because accommodation demand increases significantly before the academic year begins.
What should first-year students look for in accommodation?
Students should prioritise security, location, WiFi access, study-friendly spaces and a supportive student environment.
Is accommodation near campus important?
Yes. Living near campus can reduce travel time, lower transport costs and improve access to university facilities.
Can NSFAS students apply?
Yes. Students should review accommodation requirements and funding criteria applicable to their circumstances.
Why is first-year accommodation important?
The right accommodation can help students adapt more easily to university life and support academic success.
Is student accommodation better than commuting?
Many students find that living closer to campus improves convenience, saves time and enhances their university experience.

Apply for First-Year Student Accommodation

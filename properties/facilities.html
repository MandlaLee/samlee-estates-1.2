Core Facilities Students Should Ask About

Before choosing accommodation, students should ask what is included, what is shared and what must be provided by the student.

Room Furniture

Ask whether the room includes a bed, desk, chair, wardrobe or storage space.

Internet Access

Ask whether WiFi is included, whether it is shared and whether there are any usage rules.

Electricity and Water

Understand whether these are included, prepaid, shared or billed separately.

Bathroom Access

Ask whether bathrooms are shared, how many students share them and how cleaning is handled.

Kitchen Facilities

Ask what kitchen appliances are available and what students must bring themselves.

Laundry Arrangements

Ask whether there is laundry access on the property or nearby.

Security Measures

Ask about access control, locks, lighting, visitor rules and emergency contacts.

Transport Access

Ask how students travel to UFS, CUT, shops, libraries and daily services.

Parent Tip

Parents should ask practical questions before a student moves in. A good accommodation decision should consider safety, location, house rules, facilities, transport and total monthly cost.

Facilities and Student Success

The right facilities can help students build better routines. A quiet study space, reliable internet, a safe room and access to daily essentials can reduce stress and support academic discipline.

Students should compare accommodation based on how it will support their actual daily life, not only how it looks in photos.

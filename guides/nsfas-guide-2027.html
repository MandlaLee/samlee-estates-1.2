NSFAS Guide 2027

A complete student-friendly guide to NSFAS applications, accommodation, allowances, documents, registration and student housing for the 2027 academic year.

What Is NSFAS?

NSFAS stands for the National Student Financial Aid Scheme. It is a South African financial aid scheme funded by the Department of Higher Education and Training to support students who do not have the financial means to fund their studies. NSFAS provides financial support to eligible students at public universities and TVET colleges in South Africa.

For many students, NSFAS funding makes it possible to study, register, secure accommodation and continue their academic journey without carrying the full financial burden alone.

Important: NSFAS rules, allowance amounts and application dates can change each academic year. Always confirm final 2027 information directly through NSFAS and your institution.

What NSFAS May Cover

Depending on the applicable funding rules, NSFAS support may include tuition, accommodation or transport support, learning materials and living-related allowances.

Who Can Apply for NSFAS?

NSFAS is intended for students who meet financial, academic and institutional requirements. In general, applicants must be South African citizens or permanent residents, must have a valid email address and cellphone number, and must meet the relevant funding criteria.

Students must also be registered or intending to register for an approved NSFAS-funded qualification at a public university or TVET college. NSFAS does not fund students studying at private institutions.

Documents Students Should Prepare

Required documents may vary depending on the student's circumstances, but it is wise to prepare early.

  • South African ID document or valid identification.
  • Parent or guardian details where applicable.
  • Proof of income where requested.
  • Contact details, including email and cellphone number.
  • Academic or admission information.
  • Institutional registration information where available.
  • Accommodation documents if applying for accommodation support.

NSFAS and University Registration

Students planning to study at UFS or CUT should prepare registration documents, confirm funding status and secure accommodation early.

NSFAS Accommodation in 2027

Accommodation is one of the most important parts of student funding. Students need a safe, stable and study-friendly place to live while completing their qualification.

NSFAS uses student accommodation systems and accreditation processes to connect qualifying students with approved accommodation providers. Accommodation providers may register properties, submit required information and undergo accreditation or grading processes to confirm suitability for student living.

NSFAS Accommodation Requirements

Accommodation requirements help ensure that student housing is suitable, safe and supportive of learning. These requirements may consider property condition, safety, location, sanitation, internet access and documentation.

Students should always confirm that accommodation is properly approved or accredited before making final decisions.

NSFAS Accommodation Allowance

The NSFAS accommodation allowance helps qualifying students pay for approved accommodation. Allowance amounts and payment processes can change, so students must confirm the latest information for the 2027 academic year from NSFAS and their institution.

Students should also understand that accommodation support and transport support usually depend on where the student lives and studies.

Common NSFAS Mistakes Students Should Avoid

  • Applying late.
  • Using incorrect contact details.
  • Not checking application status.
  • Missing document requests.
  • Waiting too long to secure accommodation.
  • Choosing accommodation without checking approval or accreditation.
  • Not preparing registration documents early.
  • Ignoring official NSFAS and university communication.

What to Pack for Student Accommodation

A practical packing checklist for students moving into university accommodation, covering bedding, clothing, documents, study items, kitchen basics, laundry, safety and personal essentials.

Before You Start Packing

Packing for student accommodation is not only about clothes. You are preparing to live, study, sleep, cook, clean and manage your daily life away from home.

Many first-year students either overpack unnecessary items or forget the things they use every day. The best approach is to pack practical essentials first, then add comfort items if you still have space.

Before buying anything, ask your accommodation provider what is already included in the room. Some places provide furniture, WiFi, kitchen access or laundry access, while others may require students to bring more items.

Tip: Do not pack blindly. Confirm what your accommodation already provides before spending money on duplicate items.

Bedding and Sleeping Essentials

Good sleep matters. University becomes harder when you are tired, uncomfortable or cold.

  • Sheets
  • Pillow
  • Pillowcases
  • Duvet or comforter
  • Blankets
  • Mattress protector
  • Extra warm bedding for winter
  • Sleepwear

Bloemfontein can get very cold in winter, especially in the mornings and evenings, so warm bedding is important.

Clothing to Pack

Pack clothing that works for class, studying, weekends, church, interviews, cold weather and hot days. Avoid packing your entire wardrobe if you have limited room space.

Everyday Clothes

Comfortable outfits for lectures, campus and daily life.

Warm Clothes

Jackets, hoodies, jerseys and winter basics.

Formal Clothes

One neat outfit for presentations, interviews or formal events.

Comfortable Shoes

Shoes suitable for walking around campus.

Electronics and Power Items

Students rely heavily on electronics for communication, assignments, online learning and entertainment. Keep your electronics organised and labelled where possible.

  • Phone charger
  • Laptop charger
  • Extension cord
  • Multi-plug adapter
  • Power bank
  • Earphones or headphones
  • USB cable
  • Flash drive or external storage
  • Small desk lamp
Safety note: Do not overload plugs or extension cords. Use electrical items responsibly and follow house rules.

Toiletries and Personal Care

Pack enough toiletries for the first few weeks so you are not forced to buy everything immediately after arriving.

Bathroom Items

Soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, face cloth and towel.

Personal Care

Lotion, deodorant, hair products and grooming items.

Cleaning Basics

Toilet paper, wipes, sanitiser and small cleaning items.

Health Items

Basic medication and personal medical essentials.

Laundry Essentials

Laundry is one of the first responsibilities students underestimate. Prepare for it early.

  • Laundry basket or bag
  • Washing powder or liquid
  • Fabric softener if needed
  • Pegs
  • Hangers
  • Small sewing kit
  • Iron if allowed and needed

Kitchen and Food Basics

Ask your accommodation provider what kitchen facilities are available. Some student accommodation has shared kitchens, while some may have stricter rules about appliances.

For your first grocery trip, focus on basics like maize meal, rice, pasta, bread, eggs, tinned foods, tea, coffee, sugar, cooking oil and simple meal ingredients.

Room Organisation Items

Student rooms are often small, so organisation matters. Keep your space clean and easy to manage.

  • Storage containers
  • Hangers
  • Small bin
  • Desk organiser
  • Clothing hooks if allowed
  • Reusable shopping bag
  • Small lock if needed

Safety and Emergency Items

Safety starts with preparation. Keep important contacts saved and make sure you can respond quickly during an emergency.

Emergency Contacts

Save family, accommodation and campus contacts.

Basic Medication

Pack personal medication and basic health items.

Small Torch

Useful during power interruptions or emergencies.

Backup Money

Keep a small emergency fund separate.

Things Not to Pack

Students often bring too many items and make their rooms feel crowded. Do not bring things that create clutter, break house rules or are already provided.

  • Too many clothes
  • Large furniture unless approved
  • Unsafe electrical appliances
  • Expensive valuables you do not need
  • Duplicate kitchen items
  • Items that violate accommodation rules
  • Too much food that may spoil

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I pack first for student accommodation?
Pack your documents, bedding, toiletries, clothes, study items, electronics, medication and basic kitchen items first.
Should I bring my own bedding?
Yes, unless your accommodation clearly states that bedding is provided. Most students should bring their own sheets, pillow, duvet and blankets.
Do I need kitchen items?
Usually yes. Bring basic items like a plate, bowl, mug, cutlery, water bottle and lunch container. Confirm what your accommodation already provides.
Should I bring an extension cord?
An extension cord can be useful, but students should use it safely and avoid overloading plugs.
What documents should I bring to university?
Bring your ID, admission letter, student number, proof of registration, NSFAS information, accommodation agreement and emergency contact details.

First-Year Student Guide

A practical guide to help first-year students prepare for university life, accommodation, registration, budgeting, safety and academic success.

Welcome to Your First Year

Starting university is exciting, but it can also feel overwhelming. For many students, first year is the first time living away from home, managing money, attending lectures independently and making important decisions without daily supervision.

The goal of this guide is to help first-year students prepare properly before arriving in Bloemfontein. Whether you are studying at the University of the Free State, the Central University of Technology or another institution, your first year becomes easier when you plan early.

Accommodation, registration, funding, transport, safety and budgeting should all be handled before the pressure of the academic year begins.

Step 1: Confirm Your Admission

Before planning accommodation or travel, confirm that your admission offer is final and that you understand your programme, campus and registration requirements.

  • Check your admission status.
  • Save your student number.
  • Read your offer letter carefully.
  • Confirm your qualification and campus.
  • Check if any documents are still required.
  • Follow official university communication.

Step 2: Secure Accommodation Early

Accommodation is one of the most important decisions you will make before starting university. Your living environment affects your safety, comfort, study habits, transport costs and overall student experience.

Many students make the mistake of waiting until registration week before looking for accommodation. By then, the best options may already be taken.

Location

Choose accommodation close to your campus or transport route.

Safety

Prioritise secure accommodation with clear house rules.

WiFi

Reliable internet is important for assignments and online learning.

Study Environment

Choose a place that supports focus and academic discipline.

Step 3: Understand NSFAS and Funding

If you are funded by NSFAS, you need to understand how funding, registration and accommodation connect. Students should confirm their funding status, understand what is covered and prepare documents early.

Accommodation should not be left until the last minute. NSFAS-funded students should choose accredited accommodation and follow the correct application process.

Step 4: Create a Student Budget

University life comes with monthly expenses. Even if you receive funding or family support, budgeting helps you avoid running out of money before the month ends.

  • Accommodation
  • Groceries
  • Transport
  • Data and airtime
  • Printing and stationery
  • Laundry
  • Personal care
  • Emergency savings

Step 6: Pack Properly

First-year students often either pack too much or forget essential items. Focus on practical things that help you study, sleep, cook, clean and stay organised.

  • Bedding and blankets
  • Clothing for hot and cold weather
  • Laptop and charger
  • Extension cord
  • Study lamp
  • Toiletries
  • Laundry basket
  • Basic medication
  • Notebook and stationery
  • Reusable water bottle

Step 7: Build a Study Routine Early

First year can become difficult when students fall behind. The best strategy is to develop a routine from the beginning.

Attend Lectures

Attendance helps you stay connected to the course.

Study Weekly

Do not wait until tests before opening your notes.

Use the Library

Libraries help you focus and access academic support.

Track Deadlines

Use a calendar for assignments, tests and exams.

Step 8: Take Safety Seriously

Living independently means taking responsibility for your safety. Students should stay alert, avoid risky situations and follow accommodation and campus safety guidelines.

  • Avoid walking alone late at night.
  • Keep your phone charged.
  • Save emergency numbers.
  • Lock your room when leaving.
  • Do not share access codes unnecessarily.
  • Travel with friends when possible.
  • Report suspicious activity.
  • Follow residence rules.

Step 9: Make Friends, But Stay Focused

University is not only about academics. You will meet new people, join communities and experience independence. Friendships can make university life better, but your academic goals should remain the priority.

Choose friends who encourage growth, discipline and good decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should first-year students do first?
Confirm admission, prepare registration documents, secure accommodation and understand funding requirements.
When should I apply for student accommodation?
Apply as early as possible because accommodation demand increases before registration and orientation periods.
Is accommodation near campus better for first-year students?
Living near campus can reduce travel time, improve access to university facilities and help first-year students settle faster.
Can NSFAS students apply for accommodation?
Yes. NSFAS-funded students should apply for accredited accommodation and follow the required accommodation process.
What should I pack for university?
Pack bedding, clothing, toiletries, study materials, electronics, important documents and personal essentials.

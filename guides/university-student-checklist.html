A complete checklist to help students prepare for registration, accommodation, NSFAS, documents, packing, budgeting, safety and university life.

Before You Start University

Starting university becomes much easier when you prepare early. Many students only focus on admission, but university preparation includes registration, funding, accommodation, transport, documents, budgeting and student life.

Use this checklist as a practical guide before moving to Bloemfontein, registering at UFS or CUT, applying for accommodation, or preparing for your first year.

Tip: Do not wait until registration week to organise everything. The earlier you prepare, the easier your start to university becomes.

Accommodation Checklist

Accommodation is one of the most important decisions you will make before starting university. Your accommodation affects your safety, transport costs, study routine and daily comfort.

Location

Choose accommodation close to campus or reliable transport routes.

Safety

Check security, access control and house rules.

Study Space

Choose a place where you can focus and rest properly.

Affordability

Make sure the accommodation fits your budget or funding.

  • Apply for accommodation early.
  • Confirm whether the accommodation is close to your campus.
  • Ask what is included in the room.
  • Check if WiFi is available.
  • Ask about electricity, water, cleaning and laundry rules.
  • Understand visitor rules.
  • Confirm payment arrangements.
  • Keep a copy of your accommodation agreement.

Safety Checklist

Safety should be part of your daily routine as a student.

  • Save emergency contacts.
  • Lock your room when leaving.
  • Do not share access details unnecessarily.
  • Avoid walking alone late at night.
  • Keep valuables out of sight.
  • Use trusted transport where possible.
  • Report suspicious activity.
  • Follow accommodation rules.
  • Let someone know if you are travelling late.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should every university student prepare first?
Students should first confirm admission, prepare registration documents, secure accommodation and understand their funding status.
When should I apply for student accommodation?
Apply as early as possible before registration season, because rooms close to campus can become limited.
What documents should I keep with me?
Keep your ID, admission letter, student number, proof of registration, NSFAS information, accommodation agreement and emergency contact details.
What should I pack for student accommodation?
Pack bedding, clothing, toiletries, study items, electronics, laundry items, documents and basic kitchen items.
How do I prepare financially for university?
Create a monthly budget, prioritise accommodation and groceries, track spending and keep emergency money aside.

Student Accommodation vs Residence

A practical comparison between private student accommodation and university residence to help students and parents choose the right living arrangement for university life.

Private Student Accommodation or University Residence?

Choosing where to stay is one of the biggest decisions a student makes before starting university. Your living environment affects your safety, study routine, transport costs, independence, privacy, social life and overall academic experience.

Some students prefer university residence because it feels closely connected to campus life. Other students prefer private student accommodation because it can offer more independence, flexibility, privacy and choice.

There is no single perfect option for every student. The better choice depends on your budget, campus location, personality, study habits, safety needs, NSFAS funding position and the kind of daily routine you want.

Tip: Do not choose accommodation only because your friends are staying there. Choose the option that supports your studies, safety, budget and long-term success.

What Is University Residence?

University residence is student housing connected to the university. It is usually managed through university housing systems and follows university residence rules.

Residence can be a good option for students who want to feel close to campus life, meet other students easily and live in a structured student environment.

Campus Connection

Residence can help students feel connected to university culture and activities.

Structured Environment

Residence life usually follows formal rules and student support systems.

Student Community

It can be easier to meet other students and build friendships.

Limited Space

Residence spaces are often limited, especially for first-year students.

What Is Private Student Accommodation?

Private student accommodation is student housing provided by private accommodation providers outside direct university residence structures. It can include student houses, shared houses, apartments, private rooms, shared rooms or managed student properties.

Private accommodation is popular because students can compare locations, room types, facilities, prices, rules and lifestyle before choosing where to stay.

More Choice

Students can compare different areas, facilities and room options.

Independence

Private accommodation can help students learn responsibility and self-management.

Flexible Locations

Students can choose accommodation near campus, shops or transport routes.

Study-Friendly Living

Some students prefer a quieter environment away from busy residence life.

Cost and Budget

Cost is one of the first things students and parents compare. However, the cheapest option is not always the best option.

When comparing private accommodation and residence, look at the total monthly cost, not only the advertised rental amount.

  • Check whether WiFi is included.
  • Check electricity and water arrangements.
  • Ask about laundry access.
  • Calculate transport costs.
  • Confirm whether furniture is included.
  • Ask about deposits or once-off payments.
  • Understand payment deadlines.
  • Keep emergency money aside.

Location and Transport

Location has a major effect on student life. Living close to campus can reduce travel time, lower transport stress and make it easier to attend lectures, tutorials, library sessions and group work.

If accommodation is far from campus, students must think carefully about daily transport costs and safety.

Privacy and Independence

Privacy matters. Some students enjoy busy residence environments and shared student communities. Other students need a quieter place to focus, rest and manage their own routine.

Private student accommodation may suit students who want more control over their living space. University residence may suit students who want a more social and structured environment.

Rules and Lifestyle

Every accommodation option has rules. University residence rules are usually connected to university policies. Private accommodation rules are set by the provider.

Before choosing where to stay, ask about:

  • Visitor rules.
  • Noise rules.
  • Cleaning responsibilities.
  • Access rules.
  • Payment rules.
  • Damage policies.
  • Shared kitchen rules.
  • Parking rules.
  • Move-in and move-out procedures.
  • Quiet study hours.

Safety and Security

Safety should never be treated as a small detail. Whether you choose private accommodation or university residence, you should feel comfortable with the security, access control, lighting, rules and emergency procedures.

Access Control

Know who can enter the property and how access is managed.

Lighting

Well-lit spaces help students feel safer when moving around.

Emergency Contacts

Save accommodation, campus and family emergency contacts.

House Rules

Clear rules help protect students and keep the property organised.

NSFAS Students: What to Consider

NSFAS-funded students should be careful when choosing accommodation. If you rely on NSFAS accommodation support, make sure you understand approval, accreditation, documentation and application requirements before making a final decision.

Students should choose approved or accredited accommodation where required and keep copies of all accommodation documents.

Who Should Choose Private Student Accommodation?

Private student accommodation may be suitable for students who want independence, flexibility and more control over their living environment.

  • Students who want accommodation near UFS or CUT.
  • Students who prefer quieter living spaces.
  • Students who want to compare different locations.
  • Students who need NSFAS accredited accommodation.
  • Students who want clear provider house rules.
  • Students who want more privacy.
  • Students who want flexible room options.
  • Students who want to build independent living habits.

Who Should Choose University Residence?

University residence may be suitable for students who want a more traditional campus experience and enjoy structured student communities.

  • Students who want to be closely connected to campus life.
  • Students who prefer university-managed living structures.
  • Students who enjoy residence culture.
  • Students who are comfortable with shared spaces.
  • Students who want a more social environment.
  • Students who successfully secure residence placement early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is private student accommodation better than residence?
It depends on the student. Private accommodation may suit students who want independence, privacy and flexibility. Residence may suit students who want structured campus-linked living.
Is university residence cheaper than private accommodation?
Not always. Costs depend on location, facilities, room type and what is included. Students should compare the full monthly cost, including transport, WiFi and essentials.
Can NSFAS pay for private student accommodation?
NSFAS-funded students may use approved or accredited private accommodation where the required processes are followed.
Should first-year students choose residence or private accommodation?
First-year students should choose the option that best supports safety, study routine, budget, campus access and personal comfort.
What should I check before choosing accommodation?
Check location, safety, cost, WiFi, transport, house rules, room type, payment terms and whether the accommodation meets funding requirements.

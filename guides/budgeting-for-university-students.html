Budgeting for University Students

Learn how to manage your money, avoid common financial mistakes and make your university budget last throughout the academic year.

Why Budgeting Matters

Many first-year students receive more money than they have ever managed before. Whether your income comes from NSFAS, bursaries, parents, part-time work or personal savings, learning how to budget is one of the most valuable life skills you will develop during university.

Students who create a monthly budget often experience less financial stress, avoid unnecessary debt and are better prepared for unexpected expenses.

Know Your Monthly Income

Before you can create a budget, you need to understand exactly how much money you have available every month.

NSFAS

Allowances and approved funding.

Bursaries

Company or organisation sponsorships.

Parents

Monthly financial support.

Part-Time Work

Additional student income.

Build a Monthly Budget

Instead of spending money as it comes in, divide your income into categories before the month begins.

  • Accommodation should always come first.
  • Buy groceries before spending on entertainment.
  • Keep emergency money separate.
  • Track every expense.
  • Avoid impulse buying.
  • Review your spending every week.

Common Budgeting Mistakes

Eating Out Daily

Cooking at home usually saves money.

Impulse Shopping

Avoid buying things you do not need.

No Emergency Fund

Unexpected expenses happen.

Ignoring Small Purchases

Small daily expenses add up quickly.

Budgeting Tips for NSFAS Students

Many students receive NSFAS allowances throughout the academic year. Budgeting becomes especially important because those funds often need to last for an entire month.

  • Pay accommodation first.
  • Do not lend money you cannot afford to lose.
  • Avoid unnecessary debt.
  • Plan grocery shopping before spending elsewhere.
  • Save a small amount every month.
  • Track your spending using your banking app.

Save Money on Accommodation

Accommodation is usually the largest monthly expense. Choosing accommodation close to campus can reduce transport costs while making it easier to attend lectures and university activities.

Student Discounts

Being a student comes with opportunities to save money. Many retailers, software providers, restaurants and transport companies offer student discounts.

  • Student banking accounts.
  • Student meal specials.
  • Educational software discounts.
  • Public transport discounts.
  • Campus events.

Should You Have an Emergency Fund?

Yes. Unexpected situations such as medical expenses, travel, broken electronics or family emergencies can happen at any time. Even saving a small amount every month can make a significant difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money does a university student need each month?
Every student's situation is different. Your monthly budget depends on accommodation, transport, food, study costs and personal lifestyle.
Should accommodation be my biggest priority?
Yes. Safe accommodation should always be one of the first expenses in your monthly budget.
How can I save money as a student?
Cook at home, create a monthly budget, avoid impulse purchases and take advantage of student discounts.
Should I keep emergency savings?
Yes. Even a small emergency fund can help cover unexpected expenses.
Is accommodation near campus worth it?
Living closer to campus can reduce transport costs and save valuable time during the academic year.

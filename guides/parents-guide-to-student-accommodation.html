Parents Guide to Student Accommodation

A practical guide to help parents choose safe, suitable and study-friendly student accommodation for their children in Bloemfontein.

Helping Your Child Choose the Right Accommodation

For many parents, sending a child to university is both exciting and stressful. You want your child to study, grow and become independent, but you also want to know that they are living in a safe, suitable and supportive environment.

Student accommodation is not just a room. It affects your child’s safety, study routine, transport costs, social life, rest, discipline and overall university experience.

This guide is designed to help parents understand what to look for before choosing student accommodation in Bloemfontein, especially for students studying near UFS or CUT.

Parent tip: Do not choose accommodation based only on price. A safe location, clear rules, campus access and a proper study environment are just as important.

Location Matters

Location is one of the most important factors when choosing student accommodation. A place may look affordable, but if it is far from campus, transport costs and safety concerns can increase.

Accommodation close to campus can make it easier for students to attend lectures, access libraries, participate in study groups and return home safely after academic activities.

Safety Should Come First

Safety is usually the first concern for parents, and rightly so. Parents should ask how the property manages access, visitors, emergency situations and student behaviour.

Access Control

Understand how students enter the property and who else can access it.

House Rules

Rules help protect students and create a respectful living environment.

Lighting

Well-lit spaces help students feel safer when moving around.

Emergency Contact

Parents should know who to contact if there is a concern.

Study-Friendly Accommodation

A student needs more than a bed. They need an environment that supports studying, resting and building a disciplined routine.

Parents should ask whether the accommodation has reliable WiFi, suitable study space, clear noise rules and a living environment that supports academic focus.

  • Is the environment quiet enough for studying?
  • Are there rules around noise?
  • Is WiFi available?
  • Is there enough space for books and a laptop?
  • Are students expected to keep shared spaces clean?
  • Are there clear consequences for disruptive behaviour?

NSFAS Accredited Accommodation

If your child is funded by NSFAS, accommodation approval is very important. Parents should confirm whether the accommodation is accredited or approved for NSFAS-funded students where required.

Students should also keep accommodation documents, proof of registration and communication from NSFAS or the university safely stored.

Questions Parents Should Ask Before Choosing Accommodation

Parents should ask practical questions before allowing a child to move in. These questions help avoid confusion later.

What Is Included?

Ask about WiFi, water, electricity, furniture and laundry access.

What Are the Rules?

Ask about visitors, noise, cleaning, damages and conduct.

How Safe Is It?

Ask about access control, lighting and emergency procedures.

How Close Is Campus?

Ask about distance to UFS, CUT, shops and transport routes.

Accommodation Cost Checklist for Parents

Parents should understand the full cost of accommodation before making a decision. The cheapest monthly rent may become expensive if other essentials are excluded.

  • Monthly rental cost.
  • Deposit or once-off fees.
  • Electricity and water arrangements.
  • WiFi costs.
  • Laundry costs.
  • Transport costs.
  • Food and grocery costs.
  • Damage or breakage rules.
  • Payment deadlines.
  • Move-in and move-out terms.

Private Student Accommodation vs University Residence

Parents often wonder whether private student accommodation or university residence is better. The answer depends on the student’s needs.

University residence may offer a structured campus-linked environment. Private student accommodation may offer more independence, flexibility and choice of location.

Helping Your Child Adjust to Independence

University is a major step into adulthood. Parents should support their children without controlling every decision. The goal is to help students become responsible, disciplined and independent.

Talk to your child about budgeting, safety, communication, study discipline and house rules before they move in.

  • Agree on how often you will communicate.
  • Teach them how to manage monthly money.
  • Remind them to lock their room and protect valuables.
  • Encourage them to ask for academic help early.
  • Discuss visitor rules and personal safety.
  • Encourage healthy routines.
  • Help them understand the seriousness of accommodation agreements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should parents look for in student accommodation?
Parents should look at safety, location, house rules, WiFi, study environment, affordability, transport access and clear communication from the accommodation provider.
Is accommodation near campus better?
Accommodation near campus can reduce travel time and transport costs, while making it easier for students to attend lectures and use university facilities.
Should parents check accommodation rules?
Yes. House rules are important because they explain visitor policies, noise rules, cleaning responsibilities, payment terms and student conduct expectations.
Can NSFAS students stay in private accommodation?
NSFAS-funded students may stay in approved or accredited private accommodation where the required processes are followed.
How early should parents help students apply for accommodation?
Students should apply as early as possible before registration season, because accommodation demand increases strongly before the academic year begins.
